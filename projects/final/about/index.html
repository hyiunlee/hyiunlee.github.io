What is animal testing?



Animal testing is one method of establishing whether or not products are safe to be used by humans. They are utilized because their biology is highly similar to that of humans, not only in basic activities like breathing, digesting, and mobility, but also in basic cell processes. Furthermore, before new medications are used by humans, researchers must test them to evaluate both their positive and negative effects.


Every year, more than 115 million animals are projected to be utilized in laboratory research around the world. However, the exact number is unknown because only a tiny percentage of countries collect and publish statistics on animal testing and research. The Animal Welfare Act, or AWA, is a federal law enacted in 1966 that regulates the treatment of animals in research facilities. It mandates that certain animals raised for commercial sale, used in research, transported commercially, or shown to the public receive minimal care and treatment. However, this rule exempts approximately 95% of animals that are tested on and gives only rudimentary protections for the remainder.
